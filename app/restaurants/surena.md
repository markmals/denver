As Denver’s premiere Persian restaurant, Surena trades in the kind of slow, aromatic cooking that rewards patience, from saffron-stained rice to deeply marinated meats pulled hot from the grill. Kebabs are the anchor — juicy koobideh, tender chicken, richly spiced lamb — each plated with fluffy basmati and a charred tomato or two, but the stews are where things get especially interesting: herbaceous ghormeh sabzi, walnut-thick fesenjan with its sweet-tart edge. Portions run generous, the flavors are layered without being heavy, and a pot of hot tea at the end feels less like an add-on than part of the ritual.
